The Sound Ledger

Contribute biological witnesses to the blockchain ledger. Each recording acts as an immutable testimony to the fragile biodiversity of the Aravalli Range.

A region speaks before it is named.

The birds recorded here are not specimens — they are inhabitants. Each sound in this archive is a testimony to presence: a species in a specific place, on a specific morning, in a specific season. They are anchored to coordinates and to time.

If you have recorded a sound in the Aravallis or its surrounding ecologies — a call at dawn, a chorus at the edge of a reservoir, a single note in the scrub — upload it here. BirdNET will attempt an identification. The IUCN will locate it within a threat index.

Sign the ledger to say: I was there too. I heard this.

The Story Ledger

A repository of ecological memory — stories, warnings, and place-knowledge passed through living proximity to these landscapes.

A region is not only what it sounds like. It is also what it remembers.

Long before maps named these hills, the people who lived among them left records in a different medium — in stories passed between households, in warnings tied to the behaviour of certain trees, in the names given to springs that no longer exist. This archive is an attempt to hold some of that.

If you carry a story — heard growing up, passed down, encountered in passing — that has something to do with a place and its living world: a forest, a water body, a rock, an animal, a season, a silence — write it here. It does not need to be ancient. It does not need to be complete.

Others who know the same story, or who know the place it comes from, can sign as witnesses — not to verify, but to say: I have also heard this. This is part of what I know of that land.

The Aravallis are the frame for now. But memory does not respect administrative boundaries. Write from wherever you are listening.
